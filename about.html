History and Significance

Maletsunyane Falls is one of the most remarkable natural landmarks in Lesotho and is located near the village of Semonkong in the Maseru District. The waterfall is approximately 192 meters high and is widely recognized as one of the highest single-drop waterfalls in Africa. For many years, the falls have attracted visitors from both within Lesotho and other parts of the world who come to admire its impressive height, powerful flow of water, and the breathtaking landscape that surrounds it. The natural beauty of the area has made it an important destination for tourism and outdoor exploration. Historically, the area around Maletsunyane Falls has been closely connected to the traditions and daily life of the Basotho people. Local communities have lived near the falls for generations and have developed a strong cultural relationship with the natural environment. The name “Semonkong,” where the falls are located, means “place of smoke.” This name was given because of the thick mist that rises from the base of the waterfall as the water crashes into the valley below, creating an appearance that resembles smoke rising from the ground. The falls are not only valued for their natural beauty but also for their cultural and historical importance. Many local stories and traditions are associated with the area, and the waterfall has become a symbol of pride for the Basotho people. Over time, Maletsunyane Falls has become widely recognized as one of the key natural attractions that represent the identity and heritage of Lesotho. In recent years, the significance of Maletsunyane Falls has increased due to its role in promoting tourism and adventure activities. The surrounding area has become popular for hiking, pony trekking, sightseeing, and the famous Maletsunyane abseiling experience, which attracts adventure tourists from around the world. These activities have helped bring international attention to the region while also encouraging the protection and preservation of the natural environment. Furthermore, Maletsunyane Falls plays an important role in supporting the local economy and community development. Tourism activities in the area create opportunities for employment, small businesses, and cultural exchanges between visitors and local residents. As a result, the waterfall continues to serve as both a natural wonder and an important cultural and economic resource for the people of Lesotho.

The event brings together artists, musicians and cultural performers from across the country to showcase traditional Basotho heritage.
